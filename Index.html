Facts & Figures about Llamas

Are you crazy about llamas??? Do you count llamas instead of sheep to fall asleep? It looks like you're in the right place, then! Check out these facts to learn more about our favorite furry creature!

  • South American hump-less relative to the camel
  • Typically used as pack animals carrying anywhere from 50 to 75 pounds
  • Can cover up to 20 miles in a single day - talk about endurance!
  • Graze on grass and, like cows, regurgitate food to consume it as cud

*All facts were taken from National Geographic.

Llama vs Alpaca

This is one of the most controversial and debated topics of all time. After countless sleepless nights, I decided to to delve into this mystery and uncover the truth once and for all. Here are 6 major differences between the seemingly indistinguishable animals. Makes sure to pay attention, as there will be a quiz afterward!

  1. Ears: Llamas have longer, banana-shaped ears. Alpacas have short spear-shaped ears.
  2. Weight: Llamas can get as heavy as 400 pounds, while Alpacas generally weigh around 150 pounds.
  3. Face: Llamas have long faces, whereas an alpaca's face generally appears more blunt or smooshed.
  4. Purpose: Llamas have been bread for use as pack animals and for meat. Alpacas have been bread primarily for their fiber.
  5. Hair: Alpacas produce finer fiber than llamas. Alpacas also produce more fleece in a wider variety of colors.
  6. Disposition: Llamas are more independent minded, while Alpacas are definitely herd animals.

*All information taken from Modern Farmer.
